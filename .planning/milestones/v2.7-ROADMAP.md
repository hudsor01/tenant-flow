# Roadmap: TenantFlow v2.7 — Public Marketing Alignment

**Defined:** 2026-04-27
**Target ship:** 2026-05-08 (~2 weeks, 3 phases)
**Scope:** Close the gap between what the public marketing pages claim and what the codebase actually does. v2.6 shipped the document vault as the headline feature, but homepage/features/pricing/FAQ copy still sells the pre-demolition product (rent collection, tenant portal, smart screening). This milestone brings every public surface in lockstep with the current landlord-only SaaS reality.

## Why This Milestone Exists

A 2026-04-27 audit of public pages surfaced critical drift between marketing copy and shipped features:

1. **Stale feature claims.** Bento, how-it-works, pricing FAQ, and pricing comparison table still sell rent tracking and a tenant portal. Migration `20260418183608_demolish_rent_and_tenant_portal` removed those surfaces in April 2026; landlords now manually record amounts received in a ledger column on `leases`, not via a facilitated payment flow. "Smart tenant screening" appears in feature callouts but no screening feature exists in the codebase. Autopay is referenced via "automate 80% of tasks" but autopay was demolished.
2. **Vault is invisible.** Phases 57–66 shipped a per-entity document store with global vault, search, multi-select category filters, date-range picker, bulk-zip download, and per-owner custom categories. Zero marketing pages mention any of it. The vault is THE post-demolition headline differentiator and the public site doesn't sell it.
3. **DocuSeal tier gating is hidden in the comparison table.** PR #595 gated e-sign send-for-signature behind Growth/Max tier. The pricing comparison table reflects 3/25/Unlimited limits, but feature cards on the landing/features pages mention DocuSeal as if it's universal — buyers can't tell which plan they need without scrolling to the comparison.
4. **Unsubstantiated ROI claims.** "+40% NOI", "Reduce vacancy by 65%", "ROI in 30 days", "automate 80% of tasks" appear in feature callouts. None are backed by a published methodology or substantiated by what a landlord-only ops platform without payment automation actually delivers. Keeping unverifiable numbers risks regulatory friction (FTC ad-substantiation) and credibility with sophisticated SMB buyers.
5. **Tenants-as-users language lingers** in pricing FAQ ("how access works for teams and tenants"). Tenants are contact records post-demolition; they have no auth, no portal, no access settings. Conflating "team members" with "tenants" muddies positioning.
6. **Landlord-only positioning is implicit, not explicit.** Homepage hero says "tenants never log in" once but the features/pricing pages don't lead with it. Buyers comparing TenantFlow to multi-role platforms (where tenants self-serve maintenance requests, view payment history, sign leases) need this to be the first thing they read.

## Success Criteria

- A first-time visitor reading the homepage understands within 30 seconds that TenantFlow is **landlord-only** (no tenant portal, no tenant logins) and that the **document vault** is the headline workflow.
- Every feature claim on a public page maps 1:1 to a shipped surface in the current codebase. No "smart screening", no "automate 80%", no rent-collection-as-feature.
- DocuSeal tier limits (3/25/Unlimited) are visible on every page that mentions e-sign — not just the pricing comparison table.
- ROI / vacancy / automation percentages either (a) cite a documented methodology or (b) get removed.
- Pricing FAQ replaces "tenants" with "team members" wherever access/permissions are discussed.
- No regressions to existing E2E smoke tests; the marketing pages still render correctly across mobile + desktop breakpoints.

## Phases (strict ordering — 67 deletes stale, 68 writes new, 69 aligns pricing surface)

- [ ] **Phase 67: Demolish stale claims** — delete rent-tracking-as-feature copy, remove smart-screening claims, soften or remove unsubstantiated ROI numbers, replace "tenants" → "team members" in pricing FAQ, update FAQ entries that reference removed flows. Pure deletion / soft-edit pass; no design work.
- [ ] **Phase 68: Vault as the hero** — rewrite the homepage hero + bento grid + features page to lead with the document vault (per-entity storage, global search, multi-select filters, date-range picker, bulk-zip download, custom categories). Update Open Graph + meta descriptions. Add a vault-specific feature page section if homepage real estate isn't enough.
- [ ] **Phase 69: Pricing & e-sign tier clarity** — make DocuSeal limits visible on pricing cards (not just the comparison table). Update pricing comparison table to lead with vault features. Drop the demolished "Rent Tracking" row entirely from the comparison. Verify Stripe price IDs / plan names match the marketing copy.

## Phase 67: Demolish Stale Claims

**Goal:** Every public page reflects what the product actually does TODAY. No feature claims that depend on removed code paths.

**Audit findings driving the scope (2026-04-27 audit):**

- `src/components/landing/bento-features-section.tsx:53-59` — "Rent Tracking: Record rent received, flag late balances..."
- `src/components/sections/how-it-works.tsx:45-50` — "Track Everything: keep maintenance, **rent received**, expenses..."
- `src/app/pricing/pricing-content.tsx:29-31` and `src/components/sections/home-faq.tsx:29-31` — FAQ "How does rent tracking work?" with detailed payment-method workflow
- `src/components/pricing/pricing-comparison-table.tsx:55-63` — Full "Rent Tracking" category (6 rows)
- `src/components/landing/feature-callouts.tsx:5-6` — "Reduce Vacancy by 65%: Smart screening + marketing"
- `src/components/landing/feature-callouts.tsx:11` — "Automate 80% of Tasks: Renewals, reminders, and workflows run for you"
- `src/app/pricing/pricing-content.tsx:111` — FAQ "how access works for teams and **tenants**"
- `src/app/faq/page.tsx:36` — "+40% NOI"
- Various — "ROI in 30 days" claims

**Requirements:**

- **REQ-67-01**: Remove the "Rent Tracking" feature card from `bento-features-section.tsx`. Replace with a vault-related card OR a stronger lease-management card that doesn't claim payment facilitation. The `leases` table still has `rent_amount` and a manual ledger via `lease_reminders` — copy can describe "track rent obligations" without claiming "track rent payments".

- **REQ-67-02**: In `how-it-works.tsx` step 3, drop "rent received" from the list. Replace with a vault-related action ("upload lease + maintenance docs to the vault") or generic ("keep records the IRS asks for in one place").

- **REQ-67-03**: Delete the "How does rent tracking work?" FAQ entries from `pricing-content.tsx` AND `home-faq.tsx` (duplicated copy). Replace with a vault-focused FAQ entry ("Where do I store lease PDFs and inspection photos?").

- **REQ-67-04**: Delete the entire "Rent Tracking" category and all 6 rows from `pricing-comparison-table.tsx`. Replace with a "Document Vault" category covering the vault feature surface (per-entity storage, search, filters, bulk download, custom categories) — but keep the row count modest (max 6) so the table stays scannable.

- **REQ-67-05**: Delete the "Reduce Vacancy by 65%: Smart screening + marketing" callout from `feature-callouts.tsx`. Replace with a vault-related callout OR a substantiated landlord-ops claim that maps to actual code.

- **REQ-67-06**: Soften "Automate 80% of Tasks" — the only auto-running workflows post-demolition are the pg_cron expire_leases / payment_reminders / cleanup jobs. "Workflows" plural is fair; "80%" is unsubstantiated. Either drop the percentage or move to a verifiable number tied to actual cron-driven flows.

- **REQ-67-07**: Replace "tenants" with "team members" in `pricing-content.tsx:111` (FAQ about access). Tenants are contact records, not auth users — anywhere copy implies they have access settings, fix the language.

- **REQ-67-08**: Audit `src/app/faq/page.tsx`, `src/app/about/page.tsx`, `src/app/help/page.tsx`, `src/app/support/page.tsx`, `src/app/contact/page.tsx` for any rent-collection / tenant-portal / smart-screening / autopay references. Remove them.

- **REQ-67-09**: Soften or remove "+40% NOI" / "ROI in 30 days" / "Reduce vacancy by 65%" wherever they appear unless we can cite a methodology. Per CLAUDE.md "no half-finished implementations" applies to marketing copy too — unverifiable numbers come out.

- **REQ-67-10**: Verify `src/app/privacy/page.tsx` and `src/app/terms/page.tsx` don't mention rent collection / payment facilitation / tenant portal in ways that could create misleading legal expectations. If they do, update.

**Battle-proven criterion:** a screen-reading visitor with no prior knowledge of TenantFlow, after reading the homepage + features page + pricing page, would NOT be surprised that (a) tenants never log in, (b) the product doesn't collect rent for them, (c) the headline storage workflow is the document vault, (d) e-sign send-for-signature requires Growth or Max.

**Risks:**
- Aggressive copy deletion might leave bento cards looking sparse. Phase 68 fills the holes — but until 68 ships, the bento may be 1-2 cards short. Acceptable interim state.
- SEO impact from removing "rent tracking" / "smart screening" keywords. Re-target the affected meta descriptions in the same PR so the search-engine surface area shifts to "document vault" / "landlord operations" keywords cleanly.

## Phase 68: Vault as the Hero

**Goal:** A first-time visitor lands on `/` and the FIRST thing they see is the document vault — its capabilities, its differentiation, what it replaces (Dropbox + Google Drive + spreadsheet ledgers).

**Audit findings driving the scope:**
- The vault is mentioned ZERO times across `src/components/landing/`, `src/components/sections/`, `src/app/features/`, or any other public surface. This is the largest single positioning gap in the audit.
- The vault has six concrete differentiators: (1) per-entity organization (property, lease, tenant, maintenance, inspection branches), (2) global text search with full-text matching, (3) multi-select category filters, (4) date-range filter with timezone-aware boundary expansion, (5) bulk download as a streaming zip, (6) per-owner custom category taxonomy with seven seeded defaults.
- Each differentiator maps to a shipped phase: 57 (entity branches), 60 (global vault + search), 63 (multi-category + date filters), 64 (bulk zip), 65/66 (custom categories).

**Requirements:**

- **REQ-68-01**: Rewrite the homepage hero (`src/app/marketing-home.tsx` and the hero component it loads) to lead with the vault. Headline references documents/vault directly. Sub-headline names 2–3 of the six differentiators.

- **REQ-68-02**: Update or replace the first card in `bento-features-section.tsx` to be vault-focused. Card content covers per-entity storage + search + filters as the primary capabilities; secondary card covers bulk-zip download.

- **REQ-68-03**: Add a dedicated vault section to `src/app/features/page.tsx` (or its loader). Include a screenshot or mock UI of the vault filter row (multi-category chips + date range picker) and the row list. Section copy enumerates the six differentiators above.

- **REQ-68-04**: Update Open Graph + meta description in `src/app/page.tsx` and `src/app/features/page.tsx` to mention the document vault. Drop any references to rent collection / tenant portal in those metadata strings.

- **REQ-68-05**: Update homepage `<title>` and `<h1>` so the document vault is mentioned in the indexable text — search engines should pick up "document vault for landlords" as a primary topic.

- **REQ-68-06**: Verify the new copy doesn't claim the vault stores arbitrary file types — it stores PDFs and images per the existing storage RLS allowlist.

- **REQ-68-07**: Add `<DocumentsVault>`-style screenshots to the `public/` static assets directory. Re-use existing imagery where possible; new captures only if the existing ones don't show vault filtering.

- **REQ-68-08**: Update one home FAQ entry (`home-faq.tsx`) to answer "Where do I store lease PDFs and other documents?" with a vault-focused response.

**Battle-proven criterion:** a visitor watching the homepage scroll for 15 seconds (no clicks) reads the word "vault" or "documents" at least three times and sees one image of the vault UI.

**Risks:**
- Image production timing — if new screenshots are needed and design isn't aligned, ship copy-only changes first and add imagery in a follow-up. Don't block 68 on design.
- Hero rewriting risks SEO ranking churn for existing keywords. Mitigate by keeping current keyword-bearing strings (e.g., "landlord-only", "property management") in sub-headline / first body paragraph.

## Phase 69: Pricing + DocuSeal Tier Clarity

**Goal:** Every page that mentions e-signing also says which plan unlocks it. Pricing comparison table leads with vault, not rent tracking. Stripe metadata matches what the public sees.

**Audit findings driving the scope:**
- `pricing-comparison-table.tsx:47` shows "E-sign leases (DocuSeal): starter 3/mo, growth 25/mo, max Unlimited" — correct, but only visible if the visitor scrolls to the comparison table.
- Bento feature card and how-it-works step 4 mention DocuSeal without tier labels.
- The comparison table starts with "Rent Tracking" (already being removed in Phase 67) and "Tenant Tracking". After 67 the structural rewrite is unavoidable; 69 owns the layout.
- Pricing cards in `bento-pricing-section.tsx` need a "DocuSeal e-sign" line item with the per-tier limit, prominent enough to influence the buying decision.

**Requirements:**

- **REQ-69-01**: Add a DocuSeal limit line to each pricing card in `bento-pricing-section.tsx` (Starter: 3/month e-signs, Growth: 25/month, Max: Unlimited). Use the same per-tier number as the comparison table — single source of truth.

- **REQ-69-02**: Update bento-features-section, how-it-works, and any landing-page mention of DocuSeal to either (a) include a "Growth and Max" tier label or (b) link to pricing for tier details. No DocuSeal mention without a tier hint.

- **REQ-69-03**: Restructure `pricing-comparison-table.tsx` to lead with a "Document Vault" category (replacing the deleted "Rent Tracking" category from Phase 67). Vault category includes: per-entity storage, global search, multi-select filters, date-range filter, bulk-zip download, custom categories.

- **REQ-69-04**: Verify Stripe price metadata (`features` field, plan name, plan description) matches the public-facing copy. If any plan still has metadata mentioning rent collection or tenant portal, update via the Stripe MCP.

- **REQ-69-05**: Add a "What's new" or "v2.6 features" callout to the pricing page noting the bulk-zip download and custom categories — this is fresh value worth surfacing during the buy decision.

- **REQ-69-06**: E2E smoke test: load `/pricing`, assert each plan card mentions "DocuSeal" with a number or "Unlimited" next to it. Lock the contract so a future copy edit can't silently drop the tier label.

**Battle-proven criterion:** a visitor on `/pricing` who's already decided they need e-sign can read the pricing cards (without scrolling to the comparison table) and tell that Starter caps at 3/month, Growth at 25/month, and Max is unlimited.

**Risks:**
- Stripe price metadata edits via MCP ship to prod immediately. Stage with a test product if uncertain — don't let a copy mistake rewrite a real customer-facing description.
- Adding e-sign limits to pricing cards may require a small redesign. If shadcn `<PricingCard>` doesn't accept a "limit" line cleanly, add a generic "highlights" array prop rather than hard-coding DocuSeal.

## Sequencing Rules

1. Phase 67 ships first — pure deletion, lowest risk.
2. Phase 68 ships second — vault positioning depends on the holes 67 created.
3. Phase 69 ships last — pricing surface depends on knowing what feature copy 68 settled on.
4. Each phase ships its own PR through the perfect-PR gate (two consecutive zero-finding cycles).
5. Each PR includes a manual smoke pass: `pnpm dev`, navigate to `/`, `/features`, `/pricing`, `/faq`, scroll through each, confirm copy reflects the diff.

## Out of Scope

- Visual redesign of the bento grid or pricing cards — copy/structure changes only. If a card needs a new image, ship copy first; image follow-up is fine.
- New landing pages (e.g., `/document-vault`, `/landlord-comparison`). v2.7 fixes existing surfaces; new dedicated pages are deferred to v2.8+.
- Blog / resources content updates. The blog is its own surface with editorial cadence; not part of v2.7.
- Localization / i18n. English-only as today.
- Customer testimonials. The current testimonials section uses placeholder content; revising it is a separate trust/proof initiative.
